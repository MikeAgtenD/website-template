# Design Rules

## Global page structure

Most pages include these sections in order:
1. Header (sticky allowed)
2. Hero or page banner
3. Primary content sections
4. Optional supporting sections
5. Contact section or CTA banner
6. Footer + Subfooter

---

## Header

- Logo on the left
- Navigation links on the right
- Optional compact primary CTA button at far right
- Clean, compact, professional
- Sticky header allowed when it improves UX

---

## Hero section

- Full-width or visually strong background
- Left-aligned title (`h1`)
- Short intro paragraph
- 1–2 clear CTA buttons/links
- Must reflect the brand visually (color, shape motifs)
- Strong first impression — this is the most important section

---

## Services section

- Card-based grid (2×2, 3-col, or 4-col depending on number of services)
- Each card can include:
  - Background image or icon
  - Title
  - Short description
  - CTA link
- Cards must feel visually rich, not plain
- If multiple card-style sections appear on a page, **vary the layout rhythm** between them

---

## About section

- Image on the left, enhanced with a branded background shape or CSS decorative layer
- Content on the right:
  - Overline (small label, brand color)
  - `h2` title
  - Paragraph
  - CTA
- Tone: warm, trustworthy, structured

---

## Supporting sections (use as needed per project)

Choose from:
- Reviews / testimonials
- Process / approach steps
- Why choose us
- FAQ accordion
- Trust badges / certifications
- Before/after
- Industries served
- Team introduction
- CTA banner

**Important:** avoid stacking multiple visually identical card sections consecutively. Vary layout and rhythm to maintain visual interest.

---

## Contact section

- Title and short intro
- Contact details visible on the page (not hidden in a sidebar)
- Form layout:
  - Labels always visible (not placeholder-only)
  - Placeholders filled in with helpful examples
  - Required fields enforced
  - Honeypot field for spam protection
  - Dev-phase email: `digitalhorizonservice@gmail.com`
  - Clear success and error states
  - Accessible and keyboard-friendly

---

## Footer

Must include:
- **Left column:** logo + 1–2 sentence intro / tagline
- **Middle column:** main navigation links
- **Right column:** contact details (address, phone, email)
- **Subfooter row:**
  - Sitemap link
  - Privacy policy link
  - Optional copyright line

Privacy policy page must exist and be linked here.

---

## Service page rules

Service pages are high-priority conversion pages. Use this structure:

### 1. Top banner
- Full-width banner below header
- Clear `h1` page title
- Optional breadcrumb

### 2. Intro section
- Image with brand decorative elements on one side
- Overline + `h2` title + body text + CTA on the other side

### 3. Subservices section
- Card-based or image-backed blocks showing sub-categories or variants
- Each block: image + title + concise description
- Grid or asymmetric layout

### 4. Process / approach section
- Explain how they work step-by-step
- Use a **different layout** from the card section above (e.g. numbered steps, horizontal flow, icon list)

### 5. Additional informative content
- Text + image layout
- Benefits, applications, strengths, or differentiators

### 6. CTA banner
- Direct the user to contact / quote / appointment
- Place before the footer
- **Do NOT** place a full contact form on service pages — use a CTA banner instead

---

## About page rules

Only include an about page when strategically useful:
- Founder-led businesses
- Trust-based sectors: care, health, coaching, construction, craftsmanship, family companies
- Any business where story and credibility are key buying factors

The about page should emphasize:
- Company story and founding
- Owner or team
- Mission and values
- Why clients choose and trust them
- Local relevance
- CTA to contact

---

## Contact page rules

Recommended structure:
1. Banner/title section
2. Contact details section (address, phone, email, hours)
3. Contact form section
4. Optional location / directions section
5. Optional embedded Google Maps or Google Business link

---

## Branding and visual identity rules

Every website must visibly incorporate the client's brand elements.

### Derive styling from the logo

| Logo characteristic | Design response |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| Lines / stripes | Reuse as title accents, background stripes, SVG dividers |
| Geometric icon | Repeat shape as section accents, card borders, patterns |
| Rounded forms | Softer corners, organic blob backgrounds, rounded cards |
| Angular / sharp style | Sharp cards, diagonal section accents, clean hard edges |

### Brand color usage (must be intentional and repeated)

Apply brand primary/accent color to:
- Overline labels
- CTA buttons (background or border)
- Active nav indicators
- Highlighted keywords or spans
- Small decorative icons
- Card borders or top accents
- Section background tints
- Hover states
- Decorative shapes and SVG fills

### Anti-patterns to avoid

- Do not create a generic, colorless website
- Do not make every section look the same
- Do not use brand color only once or twice across the whole site
- Do not ignore the logo's geometry and shape language
